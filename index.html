Che-Kai Liu, 劉哲愷

I am a senior undergraduate student in Dept. Information Science and Electronic Engineering at Zhejiang University, P.R.China. I had the privilege to work with Prof. Xunzhao Yin , Prof. X. Sharon Hu and Prof. Mohsen Imani before joining Georgia Tech as a Ph.D. student in Integrated Circuits and Systems Research Lab (ICSRL), led by GT ECE chair, Prof. Arijit Raychowdhury.

My current research interests straddle computer architecture and circuit tape-out for autonomous applications. Specifically, our goal is to design beyond von-Neumann circuit and architecture for next-generation neuro-symbolic artificial intelligence with improved efficiency, resilience, and interpretability. Prior to pursuing a Ph.D. degree, my undergraduate research lies in compute-in-memory circuits and architecture, and hyperdimensional computing.

Before embarking on a computer engineering career, I was raised in Taichung and Taipei, Taiwan.

Research

Representative papers are highlighted.

COSIME: FeFET based Associative Memory for In-Memory Cosine Similarity Search
Che-Kai Liu, Haobang Chen, Mohsen Imani, Kai Ni, Arman Kazemi, Ann Franchesca Laguna, Michael Niemier, Xiaobo Sharon Hu, Liang Zhao, Cheng Zhuo, and Xunzhao Yin.
IEEE/ACM Int'l Conference on Computer-Aided Design (ICCAD), San Diego, USA, 2022
arXiv / Slides / ESWEEK Video (Youtube) / Bibtex

We propose a fully-analog nearest neighbor cosine similarity compute-in-memory fabric for ultra-low latency/energy/area applications at the edge. HDC benchmark is performed, and reported 47.1x speedup and 98.5x energy-saving over the GPU implementation.

HDGIM: Hyperdimensional Genome Sequence Matching on Unreliable Highly-Scaled FeFET
Hamza Errahmouni Barkam, Sanggeon Yun, Paul Gensler, Zhuowen Zou, Che-Kai Liu, Hussam Amrouch, and Mohsen Imani
To appear in IEEE/ACM Design Automation and Test in Europe (DATE), Antwerp, Belgium, 2023

we propose a hyperdimensional computing framework catered to highly-scaled (down to merely 3nm) multi-bit Ferroelectric FET in the context of genome sequence matching.

HDSense: Seamless Integration of Sensing with Hyperdimensional Computing
Che-Kai Liu, Hamza Errahmouni Barkam, Zhuowen Zou, Hanning Chen, Sanggeon Yun, Cheng Zhuo, Xunzhao Yin, M. Hassan Najafi, and Mohsen Imani
Under review

we propose an in-sensor hyperdimensional paradigm for compact learning at the edge. At algorithmic level, we perform extensive quantization to HD workflow. At circuit level, we propose the possibility of merging the sensor and HD encoder in the analog domain. Cross-platform comparison with eSOC, FPGA, ASIC is also performed.

XSensor: In-Sensor Autoencoder Compression for Compact Information Sensing
Mariam Issa*, Che-Kai Liu*, Hanning Chen, Sanggeon Yun, Xunzhao Yin, Arman Roohi, Shaahin Angizi and Mohsen Imani
Under review. * indicates equal contribution.

we propose an in-sensor autoencoder architecture, aiming to reduce the number of analog-to-digital interface by two orders of magnitude. Simultaneously, the one-layer autoencoder at algorithm-level is able to achieve low accuracy loss with trained decoder and deep neural networks performing on conventional platform.
